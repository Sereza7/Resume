    • Master Thesis: Mapping Online Communities of Twitch through computational linguistics

      Between January of 2021 and January of 2022, I worked on my Master's thesis on the side of the classes I took at Keio. After January 2022, my last semester at Keio was dedicated to finishing the project and redacting my thesis.
      Online communities of practice have become an important part of todayâ€™s social environment However the tools used to describe and compare them are still sparse and limited. In order to increase accessibility and discoverability of online communities of practice, I decided to study the relevance of computational linguistics when comparing communities. After building a dataset from communities found on the video streaming platform Twitch, I processed it and extracted information from this dataset using standard natural language processing tools. This way, the study ended in a network of 815 communities organised by linguistic similarity, and an unfruitful attempt at interpreting principal components as coordinates of communities on a map. After validation, the relevancy of the node diagram has been confirmed, confirming that computational linguistics can help in mapping online communities. After final consideration, it seems that computational linguistics on community vocabulary could be used to help moderate platforms and switch focus from individuals to whole communities.
